About QuickFIX
 

QuickFIX is a full-featured open source FIX engine, currently compatible with the FIX 4.0-4.4 spec. It runs on Windows, Linux, Solaris, FreeBSD and Mac OS X. API's are available for C++, Java, and .NET. Learn more.


About FIX
 

The Financial Information eXchange (FIX) Protocol is a "language" which defines specific kinds of electronic messages for communicating securities transactions between two parties. FIX defines only the format of the messages and the session-level interaction between two applications. More information on FIX can be found at the FIX protocol web site.


Open Source in Finance
 

Attending the SIMC conference on Open Source for the Securities Industry was a very surprising, and positive experience. What was interesting was the number of firms, major firms, that are actively encouraging the use and creation of open source software. For the first time when talking about QuickFIX, I didn't feel the need to start out by convincing people that open source has merit. This simple idea seemed to be generally accepted. When QuickFIX was starting out, just a couple of years ago, the attitude out there was very different.

It was nice to see that firms like Hewlett Packard, JP Morgan, Reuters, and the like are thinking about these things in a positive light. The general attitude from these companies seems to be that developers are participating in these open source communities anyway, we might as well see how we can capitalize on it. I think this is a good attitude to take because it is a tacit acknowledgement that good ideas come from all sorts of places. There is an understanding that open source projects create value even though they aren't spawned by a corporate strategist. Encouraging participation in the open source community is to encourage entrepreneurial activity from all employees.

I'm very excited to see how OSS will proliferate in finance over the next couple of years. During the month of March when we released version 1.7.0, there were 1000 downloads of QuickFIX. This is three times what we normally see for a release. During the past couple of months we have also seen many major firms that are adopting or seriously evaluating QuickFIX. In the previous corporate climate, I don't think many of these companies would have considered relying on something like QuickFIX. This is changing rapidly, and I hope we begin to see a surge in OSS participation from the financial community.
